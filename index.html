Featured Publication

Bioactive scaffolds for tissue engineering: A review of decellularized extracellular matrix applications and innovations

DOI: 10.1002/EXP.20230078

Summary: This review paper offers an extensive examination of decellularized extracellular matrix (dECM) scaffolds in tissue engineering. It examines diverse decellularization methodologies, the maintenance of original tissue architecture, and the bioactive characteristics of dECM. The research underscores the uses of dECM in engineering tissues, including skin, heart, and nerves, highlighting their promise in regenerative medicine. Issues concerning immunogenicity, scalability, and standardization are also examined, alongside prospective advancements for enhancing dECM-based scaffolds.

Decellularized extracellular matrix as a drug delivery carrier

DOI: 10.1016/j.jconrel.2025.113661

Summary: This article examines the application of decellularized extracellular matrix (dECM) as a carrier for drug delivery. It analyzes many techniques for drug loading and release from dECM, encompassing encapsulation in hydrogel formulations, direct bulk absorption of biomolecules, and affinity-based binding and conjugation. The paper additionally examines prospective uses in eye disorders and neural regeneration..
